Arithmancer is a project to track predictions and allow anyone to predict the likelihood that an event will actually happen.

Why?

Predictions matter. Whenever we make a decision, we’re modeling the future and predicting what will happen. To make better predictions, we need to collaborate, share information, and track outcomes. By creating a central record of predictions, we can aggregate individual predictions and get significantly more accurate group predictions.

How does it work?

Its a prediction market based on logarithmic market scoring rules.

How is the crowd probability computed?

It’s the current price of a single yes "vote" expressed as a percentage, which according to EMH should incorporate all of the available information.

How does scoring work?

After the outcome date has passed, you'll receive points based on the accuracy of your prediction.

If it is a prediction market why does it only ask for my likelihood estimate?

Not everyone enjoys thinking in strictly finance terms. The slider lets you submit a probability, your estimate of the likelihood of the event happening, and then using a conservative Kelly Criterion betting strategy auto calculates the quantity of Yes or No to buy or sell. This is to test whether submitting percentages encourages more participation

If there is a sell involved, the system naively sells all, as opposed to adding a limit trade. That's a TODO.
